Kelian Massa

I am an AI Engineer and Researcher at Isazi Consulting, where I work with enterprise clients to solve complex data science challenges. My main project involved building, maintaining, and deploying a large-scale transformer model for multivariate time-series forecasting. This work has led to open-source contributions and a workshop paper at NeurIPS 2024. I’ve also been fortunate to lead projects like an automated X-Ray threat detection prototype for Dubai Customs and organise a real-world forecasting hackathon at Deep Learning IndabaX South Africa.


I'm deeply passionate about harnessing Africa’s untapped talent and potential in AI. I believe that with the right tools and collaboration, African researchers and innovators can help shape globally relevant solutions. This vision drives much of my work—including organising the upcoming AI for Business and Finance in Africa workshop at Deep Learning Indaba 2025.


My journey began in 2013, building simple games under the guidance of my incredible IT teacher, Matthew Conradie, which sparked my passion for technology. I pursued Computer Engineering at the University of Pretoria, winning the best final-year project prize for a jigsaw puzzle-building robot. Supported by a bursary from AECI, I completed my MEng in the Intelligent Systems Group, specialising in 3D Computer Vision, resulting in a publication on 3D semantic segmentation of natural off-road scenes in JVCI. During my studies, I contributed to the AELMSVR project, developing a dynamic VR mesh-rendering platform in Unreal Engine.


Additional interests include reinforcement learning, particularly in intrinsic motivation and open-ended learning—how we might develop powerful, general learners akin to human children. I also have a vested interest in AI Safety & Alignment, and was fortunate to attend a 10-day AI safety training camp sponsored by AI Safety Cape Town and Condor Initiative. Finally, I'm an avid chess player and gamer, and I enjoy hiking, padel, and exploring the outdoors.
